Palak Paneer

Ingredients

  • 200g paneer, cubed
  • 300g fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2-3 tbsp cream or yogurt (optional, for creaminess)
  • Fresh lemon juice, to taste

Instructions

  1. Blanch the Spinach: Boil water in a pot, add spinach leaves, and blanch for 2-3 minutes. Drain, rinse with cold water, and blend to a smooth puree. Set aside.
  2. Cook the Aromatics: Heat oil and butter in a pan. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add Tomatoes and Spices: Add tomato puree, turmeric, cumin powder, and red chili powder (if using). Cook until the mixture thickens and oil starts to separate.
  4. Add Spinach and Paneer: Add the spinach puree to the pan and mix well. Add cubed paneer, salt, and garam masala. Stir to combine and simmer for a few minutes.
  5. Finish with Cream: (Optional) Add cream or yogurt for a richer texture. Adjust salt and add a squeeze of lemon juice for freshness.
  6. Serve: Serve Palak Paneer hot with roti, naan, or rice, garnished with extra cream or a sprinkle of garam masala, if desired.

Mutton Curry

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 2 large onions, finely sliced
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Water (as needed for gravy)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Onions: Heat oil or ghee in a large pan or pressure cooker. Add sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  2. Add Ginger-Garlic Paste: Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add Tomatoes and Spices: Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  4. Add Mutton: Add the mutton pieces, salt, and mix well. Sear the mutton on high heat for a few minutes until browned.
  5. Cook Until Tender: Add water to cover the mutton. Cover and cook until the mutton is tender. If using a pressure cooker, cook for about 4-5 whistles, then let the pressure release naturally.
  6. Finish with Garam Masala: Add garam masala and simmer for a few more minutes to blend flavors.
  7. Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve Mutton Curry hot with rice, naan, or roti.

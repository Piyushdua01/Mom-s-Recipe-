Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • For the Jamuns:
  • 1 cup Khoya (milk solids), grated
  • 1/4 cup paneer, grated
  • 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 tbsp milk (as needed for kneading)
  • Ghee or oil for deep frying
  • For the Sugar Syrup:
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods, slightly crushed
  • 1 tsp rose water (optional)
  • A few strands of saffron (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Sugar Syrup: In a saucepan, combine water, sugar, and cardamom pods. Boil until the sugar dissolves, and the syrup thickens slightly, about 8-10 minutes. Add rose water and saffron for extra flavor. Keep warm.
  2. Make the Jamun Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine khoya, paneer, all-purpose flour, and baking powder. Knead gently until smooth, adding milk if the dough is too dry. Make small, smooth balls from the dough.
  3. Fry the Jamuns: Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Gently slide a few jamuns into the hot oil, frying on low heat until they turn golden brown. Make sure to keep the oil temperature low to cook evenly.
  4. Soak in Syrup: Remove the fried jamuns and let them cool for a minute, then place them into the warm sugar syrup. Soak for at least 1-2 hours before serving.
  5. Serve: Serve the Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature, garnished with extra saffron or a sprinkle of cardamom powder.
